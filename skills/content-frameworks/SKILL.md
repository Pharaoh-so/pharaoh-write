# Content Frameworks (Always-On Essentials)

These principles apply to ALL copy, content, and strategic work. For detailed frameworks, invoke the deeper skills via the Skill tool.

## Writing Essentials

### The Novel Idea Test
Every piece must contain ideas that are:
- **Counter-intuitive** - "I never realized it worked that way"
- **Counter-narrative** - "That's not what I was told"
- **Shock and awe** - "I would have never believed it"
- **Elegant articulation** - "I couldn't have said it better"
- **Makes someone feel seen** - "That's exactly how I feel!"

If your copy doesn't hit at least one, rewrite.

### The Two-Draft Rule
1. First draft = extract ideas (write ugly, write fast)
2. Second draft = make them resonate (clarity, succinctness, intrigue)

### Rewriting Standards
- **Clear** = ideas are self-evident
- **Succinct** = everything low-value removed
- **Intriguing** = reader makes it to the end

### Voice Check
If you wouldn't say it in conversation, don't write it. No "plethora," "myriad," "seamless," "revolutionary."

## Startup/Landing Page Essentials

### The Conversion Formula
```
Purchase likelihood = (Desire × Trust) / (Labor + Confusion)
```

### Header Copy Rule
Bad headers read like slogans. Good headers describe specific outcomes.

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "Improve your workflow!" | "45 seconds to your first trade." |
| "Supercharge collaboration" | "Groceries delivered in 1 hour." |

### Market Pull Test
Ask 100 potential customers to rank 1-10 on willingness to pay. Only count 9s and 10s - 7s and 8s don't convert.

### The Six-Criteria Review
1. **Conversion** - Would you pay right now?
2. **Interest** - Did it hold attention?
3. **Clarity** - What's unclear?
4. **Expansion** - What needed more detail?
5. **Brevity** - What could be cut?
6. **Disbelief** - What felt like sales speak?

## Storytelling Essentials

### The Core Insight
**Storytelling is the art of strategically withholding information.**

Decide what to withhold until the end. Hook with incomplete information that demands completion.

### The Secret Ingredient
**Blow your own mind while telling.**

Be excited at moments of excitement. Be shocked at moments of shock. Listeners feed off this like sugar. If you're not feeling it, they won't either.

### Charisma Formula
**Confidence + Joy + Love for your audience**

Project all three simultaneously. Imagine smiling ear-to-ear internally while speaking/writing - don't show it, feel it.

### Time Dilation
Drag out climaxes. The most important moments get the most words/time. Anticipation > payoff.

## Quick Checks Before Shipping

### Copy Checklist
- [ ] Contains at least one novel idea type
- [ ] Specific numbers over vague claims
- [ ] Header describes outcome, not slogan
- [ ] Every sentence justifies its inclusion
- [ ] Would say this in conversation
- [ ] Hooks throughout (questions raised, answers delayed)

### Landing Page Checklist
- [ ] Header is specific and bold
- [ ] Value prop clear in 5 seconds
- [ ] Objections addressed before they form
- [ ] Labor minimized (fewer clicks, less reading)
- [ ] Social proof present
- [ ] Single clear CTA

### Pitch/Story Checklist
- [ ] Opens with hook (incomplete information)
- [ ] Strategic withholding (don't reveal everything upfront)
- [ ] Climax is dragged out
- [ ] Ends with meaning/lesson
- [ ] Emotionally authentic delivery
